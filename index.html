Origin of Pasta

Pasta is one of the world's most accessible foods. Nearly every country has its own unique version of this popular, inexpensive staple. In Germany and Hungry they have spaetzle. In Greeze, orzo. In Poland, they enjoy pocket-like pierogi. Ashkenazi Jewish families make kreplach dumplings. and in America, pasta os prepared and served similiarly to the way it is found in Italy - with the exception of all-American spaghetti and meatballs. The history of pasta is difficult to trace for several reasons. While we do think of pasta as a culturally Italian food, it is likely the descendant of ancient Asian noodles. A common belief about pasta is that it was brought to Italy from China by Marco Polo during the 13th century. In his book, The Travels of Marco Polo, there is a passage that briefly mentions his introduction to a plant that produced flour (possibly a bread fruit tree). The Chinese used this plant to create a meal similar to barley flour. The barley-like meal Polo mentioned was used to make several pasta-like dishes including one described as lagana (lasagna). Since Polo's original text no longer exists, the book relies heavily on retellings by various authors and experts. This, combined with the fact that pasta was already gaining popularity in other areas of Italy during the 13th century, makes it very unlikely that Marco Polo was first to introduce pasta to Italy.

Items Needed

Ingredients Measurements
Pasta 1 pack
Tomato Paste 3 Table spoon
Vegetable oil (Power Oil) 1 Sachete
Fish 2
Fresh Pepper Paste 1 table Spoon
Season Cubes 3 - 5 cubes
Salt 1 tea spoon
Onion 2 bulbs
Green peas, Carrot, Corned beef As desired

Method of Preparation

  1. Put Pasta in a pot of boiling water and allow to simmer for 8 - 10 minutes, then drain.
  2. Cut fish in bits, marinate and steam for 5 minutes
  3. In a pan, add vegetable oil, chopped Onion, tomato paste and fresh pepper paste. Then stir fry for 10 - 12 minutes
  4. Add seasoned cubes, salt and spices to taste.
  5. Add drained Pasta and continue to stir fry.
  6. Add green peas, chopped carrots and stir for a minutes, pasta is ready.
  7. In another pan fry the fish in vegetable oil.
  8. Serve Pasta with fish.
